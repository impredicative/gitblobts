Oil ends at highest since mid-December, stretching gains to an 8th straight session

By Myra P. Saefong

Published: Jan 9, 2019 2:37 p.m. ET

Share

By

MyraP. Saefong

Markets/commodities reporter

Oil futures marked their highest finish since mid-December on Wednesday, stretching their streak of gains to an eighth straight session. Trade talks that ended Wednesday between the U.S. and China were viewed as positive, easing worries over energy demand. Saudi Arabia's energy minister reportedly said he was committed to "stabilize" the oil market and recent data showed a December decline in output from the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries. The Energy Information Administration on Wednesday also reported a decline in weekly U.S. crude inventories. February West Texas Intermediate oil CLG9, +4.80% added $2.58, or 5.2%, to settle at $52.36 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
